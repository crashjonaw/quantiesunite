Pythagoras' Theorem
Sec 1-2

Pythagoras' Theorem

  • In a right-angled triangle: c² = a² + b²
  • c is the hypotenuse — longest side
  • Only works for right-angled triangles

Finding the Hypotenuse

  • c = √(a² + b²)
  • Example: a=3, b=4 → c = √25 = 5
  • The 3-4-5 triangle is the classic example

Finding a Shorter Side

  • a = √(c² - b²)
  • Rearrange the formula to isolate the unknown

Applications

  • Distance between two points
  • Ladder against a wall
  • Diagonal of a rectangle

Key Formulas

Theorem
c² = a² + b²
Hypotenuse
c = √(a²+b²)
Distance
d = √(Δx²+Δy²)

Remember

  • Always identify the hypotenuse first — it's opposite the right angle
  • Common triples: 3-4-5, 5-12-13, 8-15-17
  • Check your answer: the hypotenuse must be the longest side
